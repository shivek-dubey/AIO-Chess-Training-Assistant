King and Pawn Endgames - Opposition

Direct Opposition:
When two kings face each other with one square in between.

Example:
White King: e4
Black King: e6

The player not having the move has the opposition.

Distant Opposition:
When kings are separated by three or five squares but still maintain opposition.

Key Squares:
A king occupying a key square can force pawn promotion.

Rule of the Square:
Imagine a square from the pawn to the promotion square.
If the defending king enters the square, it can catch the pawn.

Training Tasks:
1. Practice direct opposition positions.
2. Learn key squares for all pawns.
3. Solve king and pawn studies daily.
